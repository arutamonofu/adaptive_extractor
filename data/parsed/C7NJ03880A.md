# Colorimetric Sensor of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> Based on Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>–Montmorillonite Nanocomposites with the Peroxidase Activity

Xixi Zhu<sup>a</sup>, Wei Chen<sup>a</sup>, Kaili Wu<sup>a</sup>, Hongyu Li<sup>a</sup>, Min Fu<sup>a</sup>, Qingyun Liu*<sup>a</sup>, Xiao Zhang<sup>b</sup>

### Abstract

Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles deposited on montmorillonite (Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs) were synthesized via a facile method and characterized by energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS), powder X-ray diffraction (XRD) and transmission electron microscopy (TEM). Significantly, the as-prepared Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs were demonstrated to possess an intrinsic peroxidase-like activity that can rapidly catalytic oxidize peroxidase substrate 3,3’,5,5’-tetramethylbenzidine (TMB·2HCl) by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>, accompanied with a visible color change (colorless to blue), which developed a sensitive colorimetric sensor for detecting H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. The catalytic reaction of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs followed the Michaelis-Menten kinetics equation, and the composites showed good affinity towards TMB. The proposed method can detect H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> in the range of 10-100 μM with a low detection limit (DL) of 8.7 μM. Fluorescent data revealed that the electron transfer among catalyst, TMB and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> may contribute to the peroxidase-like activity of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs. Finally, a colorimetric method for detecting H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> was proposed, and the robustness of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs was pretty good. The sensor of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> based on Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs can be found to have great potential applications in the medical, food and environmental fields.

**Key words:** Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT; nanocomposites; peroxidase; H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>; colorimetric

### 1. Introduction

As an important chemical substances, hydrogen peroxide (H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>) has been widely used in textile industry, chemical industry, paper industry, food industry, and so on. Moreover, H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> is a kind of intermediate in some metabolic reaction of human bodies, including glucose oxidation, trioxypurine oxidation, acetylcholine decomposition, etc. On this basis, it is possible to detect target object (glucose, trioxypurine, and acetylcholine) though measuring the concentration of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> indirectly. In addition, H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> also exists in some food, seawater and waste water. Thus, it is necessary to develop a convenient and facile method to determine the concentration of these micromolecules, such as H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> and acetylcholine [1-3].

Compared with traditional methods, such as electrochemistry [4], chemiluminescence [5], liquid chromatography [6], fluorescence [7] and bioelectrochemistry [8], the colorimetric assay attracts more attention due to its low-cost, timesaving, sensitivity and pretty simplicity [9]. Colorimetric detection technologies provide a valuable set of approaches suitable in routine tests for environmental monitoring and biomedical diagnosis purposes.

As a natural peroxidases, horseradish peroxidase (HRP) can catalyze the oxidation of some substrates (ascorbate, ferrocyanide, catochrome C and the leuco form of many dyes, etc.) in the presence of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> [10]. Thus, HRP is the most common enzyme for H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> sensing. However, the application of HRP has been limited due to the complicated process of preparation and purification, as well as easy inactivation under extreme conditions, including basic medium, strong acidic and high temperature [11]. It is amusing that the magnetic Fe<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles possess the peroxidase-like activity, which could be used as mimic enzymes to replace HRP [12] Since then, various nanomaterials were investigated as peroxidase, such as Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> NPs [13], TCPP-NiO NPs [14], Fe–Co NPs [15], CNPs [16], etc.

As we know, nanoparticles are tend to aggregate, due to the relatively smaller particle size with high interface energy, resulting in the decrease of catalytic activity to some extent. Thus, some supports are necessary to prepare the nanocomposite, in order to maintain the catalytic activity of small nanoparticles. Montmorillonite (MMT) is one kind of phyllosilicate minerals composed by one layer of aluminum-oxygen octahedron and two layers of silicon-oxygen tetrahedron, and it has been widely applied in separation, catalysis, optical and electrical devices due to layered structure, large specific area and high cation exchange capacity [17]. There are many reports on MMT based materials, included inorganic materials and organic materials, such as ZnO-MMT, CeO<sub>2</sub>-MMT, CuS-MMT, and Polyethylene-MMT etc [18-24]. From these reports, it can be found that MMT plays a key role in the dispersion of other components, and then prevents the aggregation of the nanoparticles. With these ideas in our mind, the combination of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> and MMT is attractive to construct an efficient nanocomposite as enzyme mimic. So far as we know, there is no relevant report about Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs applied in H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> detection.

Herein, Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites were prepared through a one-step hydrothermal route and investigated as efficient and novel peroxidase mimics. Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles were homogeneously distributed on the MMT sheets. Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs possessed the catalytic activity of peroxidase and weak oxidase, and showed typical Michealis-Menten kinetics and good affinity toward TMB. The peroxidase-like activity of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs may originate from the electron transfer among catalyst, TMB and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. The fluorescent technology was employed to verify the catalytic activities of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs and to support the mechanism analysis. Based on the peroxidase activity, sensitive colorimetric sensors of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> was designed.

### 2. Experimental section

#### 2.1 Reagents

Co(NO<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O, acetic acid (HAc), hydrogen peroxide (30% wt%, H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>) and sodium acetate (NaAc)were obtained from Beijingchemical Reagent Factory (Beijing, China). 3,3,5,5-Tetramethylbenzidine (TMB) and Montmorillonite (MMT) were obtained from Solarbio (Tianjin, China). Terephthalic acid (TA) was obtained from Chengdu cologne chemical reagent factory. The chemicals used in this study were of analytical grade. All solutions were prepared with De-ionized water.

#### 2.2 Characterization

The X-ray diffraction (XRD) spectra were obtained using a Rigaku D/Max2500PC diffractometer with graphite monochromatized Cu K$\alpha$ radiation. The elemental compositions of the composites were identified using energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDS). The morphology and shape of the nanocomposites were imaged by the transmission electron microscope (TEM, JEM-2100, JEOL, Japan). UV–vis absorption spectra were measured on a Hitachi UH4150 spectrophotometer (Japan). Fluorometric measurement was carried out by a Hitachi F-4600 fluorescence spectrophotometer (Japan).

#### 2.3 Preparation of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites

Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites were prepared as follows: Firstly, 0.3000g MMT was dissolved in 13mL alcohol aqueous solution (H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>:C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>5</sub>OH=1:1) under stirring violently for 18h to form a homogeneous suspension. Secondly, 0.2910g of the Co(NO<sub>3</sub>)<sub>2</sub> (1mmol) were added into the above suspension under vigorous stirring for another 2h. The resulting solution was assigned as solution A. Thirdly, 1.25 ml ammonium hydroxide was cautiously dropwise added into the solution A and stirred for 1h. Subsequently, the above solution was sealed in an autoclave and heated at 170 °C for 8h. Finally, the products were collected by centrifugation, washed with distilled water for three times and subsequently ethanol, and then dried at 60 °C for 8 h in air.

#### 2.4 Peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites

The peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites was investigated via the results of catalytic oxidizing the substrate TMB by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. In a typical procedure, 0.2 mL of TMB (1 mM) in sodium acetate buffer solution (pH=4.0) was dealt with different reaction systems, listed in Table 1. The total reaction volume was 2 mL. The absorption spectra of different experimental systems were measured by recording the absorption spectra in wavelength mode (500-800nm).

#### 2.5 Kinetics measurements

The kinetic measurement of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites was executed on a UV-vis absorbance spectrophotometer under time-scan mode. The experiment process was as follows: 0.3 mg mL<sup>−1</sup> Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites as enzyme mimics in HAc-NaAc buffer solution (pH=4.0) with 0.2 mL of TMB (1mM) and 0.2 mL of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> (10–1000 mM) as a substrate, or 0.2 mL of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> (0.25 M) and 0.2 mL of TMB (0.1–1 mM) as a substrate, and keep total reaction volume was 2mL. The Michaelis–Menten constant (K<sub>m</sub>) could be obtained according to the Lineweaver-Burk plot: $1/v = K_m/V_{max} (1/[S] + 1/K_m)$, where v, V<sub>max</sub>, [S] and K<sub>m</sub> is represent the initial velocity, the maximal reaction velocity, the concentration of the substrate, and the Michaelis constant, respectively [25].

#### 2.6 Detection of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> using Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites

The experiments of colorimetric detection of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> with Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites as catalysts were performed as follows: using TMB (0.2 mL, 1 mM) as peroxidase substrate in the presence of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> (0.2 mL) and 0.2 mL Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs (0.3 mg·mL<sup>−1</sup>), together with 1.4 mL sodium acetate buffer solution (pH=4.0). The catalytic reaction of TMB oxidized by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> in the presence of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs was investigated according to the absorbance of the resulting solution.

From the Fig.1c, besides the above characteristic peaks of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>, the peak appears at 7.89° can be indexed as (001) plane of MMT, suggesting that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles were deposited on the surface of MMT, which can be observed in the subsequent TEM images.

The morphologies of the MMT, Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> NPs and Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites were imaged by TEM, shown in Fig. 2. It can be seen from Fig. 2b that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles without MMT as support easily aggregated. However, as shown in Fig. 2c, the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles with ca. 10-20 nm in size were dispersed well on the surface of MMT sheets, suggesting that MMT was an ideal support for the growth of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles.

In order to determine the influence of the reaction time on the preparation of nanocomposites, we have carried out the experiments for different reaction time (2h, 4h, 6h, 8h, 10h). And the obtained nanocomposites were characterized by XRD and SEM, shown in Fig. S1 and Fig. S2 (supporting information), respectively. From the XRD patterns, it can be seen that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles have been formed after reaction for 2h. The diffraction peaks at 19.04°, 31.32°, 36.89°, and 44.87° agreed well with that of peak of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> (JCPDS card no.42-1467). Moreover, from the XRD data, it can be found that the longer the experimental time, the more the intensity of the peaks of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles. It can be found that when experiments have carried out for 8 h, the intensity of diffraction peaks of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles have reached maximum. However, it can be also found that the XRD peaks of MMT is hardly any changed.

From SEM images shown in Fig S2 (supporting information), it can be seen that nanocomposites, which have been prepared under the condition of reaction for 8 h, dispersed well on the surface of MMT. Nevertheless, when the reaction have sustained for 10 h, it can be found that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles have agglomerated on the surface of MMT to some extent.

On the basis of the results described above, it can be indicated that Montmorillonite provide a good supporter for Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> growing, but the influence of Montmorillonite on the XRD of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> is very limited.

#### 3.2 Peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites

The peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites was investigated via the results of catalytic oxidizing TMB by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. TMB was oxidized into an intermediate product with an obvious color change from colorless to blue corresponding to a strong absorption band at 652 nm, and this process could be observed by the naked eyes. From Fig. 4, it can be seen that the absorbance of system (a) is higher than that of other systems, indicating that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites possessed the enhanced the peroxidase catalytic activity. It also can be found that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles (b) possessed the peroxidase activity, which is in accordance with the previous report [13], weaker than that of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites, suggesting that MMT as a good supporter played an important role in dispersion of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles. That is to say, MMT increases the dispersity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles, and the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites had more active sites with substrate than that of sole Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub> nanoparticles. By contrast, the experimental system without Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites (d) exhibited a negligible color change. It can be concluded from system (e) that sole MMT was not demonstrated the peroxidase activity. System (f) shows no obvious color change, indicating that Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites are required for the catalytic oxidation reaction. Interestingly, system (c) demonstrated a wathet blue change, indicating the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites possessed weak oxidase-like activity. Overall, Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites exhibited peroxidase- and oxidase-like catalytic activity under identical conditions. It is the di-enzymes effects that accelerate the oxidation of TMB and result in the rapid response for H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. Subsequently, we emphasized to study the peroxidase-like properties of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT, because the oxidase-like activity is relatively weak.

#### 3.3 Experimental condition optimization

Similar to natural peroxidases and the previous reported nanomaterial-based peroxidase mimics [27-29], the catalytic activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs depends on pH and temperature, respectively. Changes of the pH (1.7-9.0) and the temperature (20 - 60°C) were executed to evaluate the influences of pH and temperature on catalytic activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs, respectively.

As shown in Fig.5a, the catalytic activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites first increased untill pH=4.0 and then declined with pH value increases. Thus, the optimal pH was approximately 4.0, revealing that this reaction occurred easily in weak acidic solutions, which was consistent with the previous reports [14, 20]. Fig.5b shows temperature dependence of catalytic activity. The highest catalytic activity was obtained at approximately 40°C, indicating that the optimal temperature was 40°C, which was consistent with porphyrin functionalized ZnS nanoparticles [30].

#### 3.4 Kinetic studies of the peroxidase-like activity of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs

It is known that the catalytic oxidation of TMB by many enzymes mimetics follows the Michaelis-Menten behaviors. Michaelis-Menten equation ($v = V_{max}S/(K_m + S)$) as one of the earliest descriptions of the enzymes action, can reveal the relationship between substrate concentration (S) and initial rate (v). Series of assays were carried out with the change of one substrate concentration, and kept the other conditions fixed at the same time, so as to further inveatigate the peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs. Typical Michaels-Menten curves of TMB and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> were obtained by plotting concentration against the initial reaction rate, shown in Fig. 6.

![Kinetic analysis](Kinetic_analysis.png)
**Fig. 6.** Steady-state kinetic analysis utilizing the Michaelis-Menten model (A, B) and Lineweaver–Burk double-reciprocal model (C, D) for Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites. The concentration of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> was 0.25 M while TMB concentration was altered (A, C). In turn, the concentration of TMB was 1 mM and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> concentration was varied (B, D).

Michaelis constant (K<sub>m</sub>) is often related to the affinity between the substrate and the catalyst obtained by the Lineweaver-Burk (L-B) plot. The smaller the value of K<sub>m</sub>, the stronger the affinity between the substrate and the catalysts. Comparison of maximum reaction rate (V<sub>max</sub>) and the apparent Michealis constant (K<sub>m</sub>) between Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs and HRP were illustrated in Table 2. It can be seen that the K<sub>m</sub> value for Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs with TMB as the substrate is calculated to be 0.002163 mM, which was 20 times lower than that for HRP enzyme, indicating that the stronger affinity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs towards TMB than that of HRP. However, the K<sub>m</sub> value of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs with H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> as substrate is 20.492 mM, higher than that of HRP.

#### 3.5 Mechanism of the peroxidase-like activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs

In order to figure out the catalytic mechanism of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites, a series of experiments were carried out. In these experiments, terephthalic acid was employed as a fluorescence probe to replace TMB and estimate the effects of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs on [·OH] radicals. Terephthalic acid easily captured [·OH] radicals to form 2-hydroxy terephthalic acid which can generate high fluorescent intensity [33]. The typical assay was performed using 10mM H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>, 0.5mM terephthalic acid and the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites with different concentration were incubated in HAc-NaAc buffer solution (pH=4.0, 100 mM) at 40 °C for 40 min.

The fluorescent results were displayed in Fig. 7. It was observed that the fluorescence intensity gradually decreased with the amount of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs increased, which was inconsistent with that of some previous works [14, 30]. In other words, the higher concentration of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites, the less [·OH] radicals produced, suggesting that the composites inhibit the production of [·OH] radicals. According to the relevant publications [34-35], this kind of efficient catalytic performance may originate from the electron transfer among catalyst, TMB and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. On the basis of experimental results and relevant reports, we proposed the catalytic mechanism of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites, illustrated in Scheme 1. The higher catalytic activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites may be explained due to transfer of electrons from the top of the valence band of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites to the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. Moreover, the amino groups of TMB donate lone-pair electrons to Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites, which confers an increase in electron density and mobility in Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites. This transfer of charges increases the electro-chemical potential from the LUMO of H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> and therefore helps the electron transfer process from Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT composites to H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> [36].

#### 3.8 Robustness of peroxidase activity of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs

As we know, the stability of the catalyst is of great importance for the following application. The temperature and pH are the most common impact factors on the structure and catalytic activity of catalysts. According to the reference [12], the catalytic activity of the HRP dramatically decreased after being treated at different temperatures and pH values for 2 hours. Based on the similar method, the stability of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs were studied in this paper. The activity of the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs were measured under optimum conditions (pH 4.0, 40 °C) after being incubated at different pH values (2-10) and temperatures (0-80 °C) for 2 h, shown in Fig. 9 and Fig. 10, respectively. From the data, it can be seen that there are no obvious changes of Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT activity after incubating in different pH values and temperatures, and the lowest relative activitiy are exceed 90%, indicating that the Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs possesses good stability under a widely range of pH values form 1 to 10 and temperatures from 0 to 80 °C, respectively.

### 4. Conclusion

In summary, this work reported a facile method to synthesize Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT nanocomposites by one-step hydrothermal route, and this nanoparticles were demonstrated to possess intrinsic peroxidase-like activity which could catalytic oxidize TMB by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> accompanied with visible color change by naked eyes. The Co<sub>3</sub>O<sub>4</sub>-MMT NPs exhibited higher catalytic activity than pure MMT and the catalytic capacity was strongly dependent on temperature and pH. The results showed that the catalysis followed typical Michaelis-Menten kinetics and had a strong affinity to TMB. This kind of efficient catalytic performance may derive from the electron transfer among catalyst, TMB and H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>. Based on the results described above, a simple, colorimetric and visual method for detecting H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub> was established, which had a promising application in food detection, biochemical analysis and environmental monitoring.
